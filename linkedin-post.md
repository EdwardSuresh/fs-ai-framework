Every AI governance framework tells you WHAT to care about.

Almost none tell you what the Architecture Review Board should actually ask for.

After 25+ years architecting in financial services, I built the missing layer — and published it.

**FS-AI Framework: 14 AI architecture principles + a reference architecture for Capital Markets, Wealth Management & Insurance.**

This is not another ethics list. Every principle is written in TOGAF form (Statement, Rationale, Implications), derived from real obligations — SR 11-7 model risk, Reg B adverse action, books-and-records, NAIC's AI bulletin, MiFID II algo controls — and traced to the architecture component that enforces it and the evidence an ARB should demand.

What's inside:

▪ 14 principles across Trust & Accountability, Risk & Compliance, Data Integrity, and Resilience
▪ A layered reference architecture — with controls in an independent control plane the model can't touch
▪ A traceability matrix: principle → component → ARB evidence
▪ A crosswalk to NIST AI RMF, ISO/IEC 42001, and the EU AI Act
▪ Field notes and nine named anti-patterns — "The Silent Vendor Update," "The Proxy in the Data," "The Helpful Hallucination"

The design thesis: in regulated finance, the question is never "what can AI do?"

It's "what can we stand behind?"

Govern the model like a trader. Document it like a record. Design it to withstand scrutiny — because one day it will face it.

Built for the client. Defensible by design.
