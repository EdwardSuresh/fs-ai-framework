Design thesis

The architecture is the principles made physical. Three structural decisions follow directly from the catalog: (1) controls live in an independent control plane the models cannot modify (FS-AI-08, 12, 13); (2) records/lineage and observability are cross-cutting services touching every layer, not features of individual systems (FS-AI-06, 14); (3) a human oversight layer sits between AI services and clients wherever advice, money, or adverse decisions are involved (FS-AI-02, 05).

Reading the diagram

Requests flow top-down: channels → human oversight → AI services → control plane → knowledge → data. Nothing reaches data except through the boundary; nothing reaches clients except through oversight and controls. The cross-cutting services on the right capture lineage and telemetry from every layer. Principle references [FS-AI-nn] mark what each element enforces.

Architecture Building Blocks

ABB catalog · vendor-neutral

Each block is described by purpose, capabilities, and the principles it realizes. Indicative realizations are illustrative only — the architecture is deliberately platform-agnostic (e.g., an AI service layer could be realized with Azure AI Foundry, the Claude API, or equivalent platforms behind the same control plane).

L1

Governed Data Boundary

realizes FS-AI-09

The perimeter inside which all AI data processing occurs. Nothing classified as client, account, policy, MNPI, or proprietary leaves it toward unapproved services.

  • Data classification and entitlement enforcement at every AI access path
  • DLP policies specific to AI endpoints (prompt and file inspection)
  • MNPI / information-barrier zoning; residency-aware deployment zones
  • Vendor controls: no-train clauses verified, segregation, deletion rights
ARB checks: approved-service catalog exists; DLP rules tested; vendor attestations on file.
L2

Knowledge & Grounding Services

realizes FS-AI-10

The only sources client-facing and decision-supporting AI may speak from: owned, current, and citable.

  • Governed repositories (filed policies, approved research, product data) with named owners
  • Retrieval index with access control inherited from source entitlements
  • Citation service attaching sources to generated outputs
  • Freshness SLAs; stale content flagged or excluded from retrieval
ARB checks: knowledge owners assigned; hallucination test results in validation pack.
L3

Independent AI Control Plane

realizes FS-AI-08 · 12 · 13

The architectural heart of the design: limits, gates, and shutdown live here, outside the reach of any model or agent. A model can propose; only the control plane permits.

  • Policy gateway and guardrails on every AI request/response path
  • Confidence-based routing: low-certainty cases go to humans by design
  • Pre-trade risk limits, throttles, and tested kill switches (CM overlay)
  • Compliance gates wired into promotion pipelines; safe-fallback orchestration on failure
ARB checks: kill switch test evidence; fallback runbook per workflow; gate bypass impossible by construction.
L4

AI Services Layer

realizes FS-AI-03 · 05

The models and assistants themselves — all of them registered, tiered, and validated before this layer serves anything real.

  • GenAI assistants (RAG pattern by default), predictive models, recommendation engines, agentic workflows
  • Explainability services producing reason codes / key factors for adverse decisions
  • Every service bound to an inventory entry, owner, and tier
ARB checks: inventory completeness; validation report matching tier; explainability output samples.
L5

Human Oversight & Licensed Review

realizes FS-AI-02 · 04 · 05

The layer that keeps accountability human. Advice, adverse decisions, and judgment calls pass through here before they touch a client.

  • Licensed approval workflows: AI drafts, humans adopt and own
  • Escalation queues fed by confidence routing and client-risk triggers
  • Four-eyes checks for high-impact decisions; suitability constraints applied upstream of recommendation display
ARB checks: supervision procedures reference AI flows; approval audit trail joins the lineage service.
L6

Experience & Channels

constrained by FS-AI-02 · 10

Where users meet the system. Channels render AI output with its provenance and its limits made visible.

  • Advisor workspace, client portal/chatbot, trader desktop, claims workbench, producer portal
  • Standardized AI labeling and disclosures; citations rendered with content
  • No channel talks to models directly — all traffic transits the control plane
ARB checks: disclosure copy approved by Compliance; channel-to-model direct paths do not exist.
X-Cut

Records & Lineage Services

realizes FS-AI-06 · 11

Shared, cross-cutting capture of every decision and every version, replayable years later.

  • Decision logging standard: inputs, data versions, model/prompt versions, output, human action
  • Model/prompt version registry; vendor model versions pinned and recorded
  • Immutable archive aligned to retention schedules; reconstruction API tested in audits
ARB checks: a sampled past decision is successfully reconstructed end-to-end.
X-Cut

Observability & Assurance Services

realizes FS-AI-07 · 14

Cross-cutting telemetry turning operation into assurance: performance, drift, fairness, anomalies.

  • Per-model dashboards: volume, automation rate, escalation rate, error rate
  • Data/concept drift detection with thresholds set at validation time
  • Production fairness monitoring for in-scope models; surveillance feeds for AI-driven flow
  • Alert → review → restrict → retrain → retire playbook
ARB checks: thresholds documented; a drift alert drill has been executed.
Plane

Governance Plane

realizes FS-AI-01 · 05 · 08 · 11

The operating model wrapped around everything: who owns, who validates, who approves, who changes.

  • ARB gates at intake, design, go-live, and material change
  • Model inventory and tiering; independent validation function with restriction authority
  • Change management covering models, prompts, agents, and vendor updates
ARB checks: the governance plane is itself audited — gate records, waiver registry with expiry dates.

Adoption & Roadmap

5W & 1H · phased build

How an institution adopts this reference architecture — and the order in which to build it, since the cross-cutting services and boundary must exist before AI services scale.

Phase 1 · Foundations
Boundary + Cross-Cutting Services first

Stand up the governed data boundary (L1), records/lineage and observability cross-cutting services. Establish the model inventory and ARB gates. Nothing client-facing yet — this is what makes everything later defensible.

Phase 2 · Controlled Value
Control plane + first use cases

Deploy the independent control plane (L3) and human oversight workflows (L5). Launch internal, human-in-the-loop use cases per sector (advisor assistant, claims triage, pre-trade analytics) as governed pilots.

Phase 3 · Scale & Specialize
Sector overlays + automation depth

Add sector overlays (execution limits, suitability constraints, straight-through claims), expand confidence-routed automation, and operationalize the drift/fairness playbooks at portfolio scale.
